Portugal wedding photography

12 years of full-time experience in destination wedding photography. I still decided not to pursue it here — because the math would not work out.

  • Analysed the local competition
  • Found the prices of all top wedding photographers
  • Sized the market: weddings per year, seasonality
  • Listed potential costs: travel, equipment, ads, taxes

Why do this before building or testing anything?

  • People driven by an idea hit a WALL — sooner or later — on problems they could have easily foreseen
  • Skip this and you can spend months chasing the wrong goal, and get demotivated
  • It's a free market: conviction matters, but demand and unit economics can be foreseen
  • Be cold-hearted. Don't get married to your first idea
  • If the math is not mathing — adjust or pivot until it sings

Either way, two numbers rule everything:
CAC — cost to acquire a customer · LTV — lifetime value of a customer

VC-backed vs bootstrapped

  • Why raise money? To build an MVP (hire a team)? That cost trends to $0 now — except hard tech
  • For growth? Investors won't fund acquisition unless you already have hockey-stick growth
  • Investors do not invest in ideas. VCs are only interested in $1B+ outcomes
  • Good news: there are plenty of ways to build a viable business without raising a cent

How aware is your customer?

Eugene Schwartz · The further right your customer sits, the more direct your marketing can be. A graduate googling "ai headshot generator" is already solution-aware.

Do not skip this step

People have built entire working products without ever checking the competition — when it was clear they would not stand a chance.

  • Google your terms: "ai headshot generator" · "linkedin headshot ai" · "ai professional photos"
  • Ask an AI chat: "I want a professional LinkedIn headshot without a photographer — how?" See what it recommends
  • Broad rivals: ChatGPT, Canva, Adobe  ·  Niche rivals: instaheadshots.com, aragon.ai, headshots.com

Google "ai headshot" — every result on page one is a paying competitor.

Location is very important

  • Pick a target location — you cannot target "worldwide"
  • Tier 1 countries (USA, Canada, UK…) ≠ everywhere else: different population, purchasing power, regulations
  • Research with a country VPN and in that country's language — you'll see what their customers see

CAC: what one paying customer costs you

Almost every business underestimates this. Drag the conversion rate and watch the CAC move:

$8 per click ÷ conversion rate. At 1% → $800 per customer. At 5% → $160. At 15% → ~$53.

Same product, very different CAC

Don't forget creative prep costs — and that organic can be ~$0 if one video goes viral, or ∞ if none do.

What you'll actually spend

  • CAC — usually the biggest line by far
  • Hosting, tools, etc. Usually small
  • AI costs — generating the images
  • Creating and managing ads and creatives

What can you charge?

  • Business model: one-time purchase. Competition charges $49–$75
  • LTV = average total a paying customer spends over their lifetime. One-time purchase → LTV ≈ the price
  • Value-based pricing: how important is the problem? (Find a better job → make more money!)
  • We'll make the best generator and charge $99, because our smallest CAC is $75

If CAC > LTV, you will never make money.

Improve conversion

Better ad creatives · landing page · onboarding · paywall

Change the field

Different market · different location · different language
